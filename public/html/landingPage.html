Grümr

(pronounced like groomer)

Grümr is an app designed to connect those with dogs in need of a groom and the grooming salons in the area.


There were multiple inspirations for this app. My lovely girlfriend is a dog groomer, so I was given a behind-the-scenes look at the grooming industry. What I found was that, even though technology has advanced to the point where online scheduling is commonplace, most grooming salons still schedule all appointments by phone calls and hand-written schedules.


The reason behind this is relatively simple. Customers don't know how long a groom will take. The one who schedules the groom over the phone is usually a groomer themself, so he/she knows how much time needs to be blocked off for each service/dog. Lucky for me, I live with a groomer and she is able to give me insight on the time requirements for each groom.


Originally, the plan was to add an online scheduling page onto the grooming salons' existing websites. Then I realized that would limit the potential for this app.


In 2009, Chris Jeffery and Jason Kwicien created an online food ordering system which they deployed to serve State College, PA. It was called LionMenus, though it changed to LocalUp later that year when they relocated to Baltimore. Today, OrderUp (rebranded as such in 2012) is active in 37 cities nationwide and was recently acquired by Groupon for a respectable $69 Million.


The final product will be similar to OrderUp. Businesses will pay to be listed on the website, customers will pay a small service fee as a part of their final bill.


Click the button below to demo the app. I recommend creating a user profile to check out what it's like as a customer. No need to put in a real email address; there is currently no validation as this is just a demo.


Try adding in your dog, then scheduling an appointment with one of our fake salons.


Damn-It Score Card

Damn-It is a fun game. My parents taught me when I was very young, and I don't know if "Damn-It" is its official name, but it's a fun game.


Recently, my girlfriend's younger siblings came for a visit. As they are almost college aged, I decided to show what cool things you could do as a coder.


We had to go get them in the early evening, so in the afternoon I built this score card app. There are 12 levels you need to pass in order to win, so each round it displays the rule you are currently on.


I also figured if any of my family still plays they'd enjoy this score card. Normally, it can get confusing remembering what you (and your opponents) are currently working on, so this should help them. I hope you all enjoy the app!
